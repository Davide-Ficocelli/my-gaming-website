About the game

Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty is a stealth action game developed by Konami Computer Entertainment Japan and published by Konami. Released in 2001 for PlayStation 2, it follows rookie agent Raiden as he infiltrates an offshore cleanup facility to rescue hostages from a terrorist group. The game features revolutionary graphics for its time and a complex narrative exploring themes of information control, artificial intelligence, and digital legacy.

  • Complex narrative with philosophical themes
  • Varied environments from tanker to plant
  • Multiple equipment options

Why I like it

MGS2 blew my mind with its meta-commentary on sequels and player expectations. The bait-and-switch from Snake to Raiden was controversial but brilliant, making me question the connection to digital characters. This is one of those games I appreciate more as I get older. The metal gear saga is one of those which details are not caught in the first playthrough. In fact I was at first confused by the story, but with further research and rewatches I realized how deep Kojima's writing is. The uncanniest part of this game for me was the pre-final boss speech of the patriots's AI. As I was listening, it was easy for me to forget that this game was released in 2001 because of the scary accuracy of the AI's speech about the digital age as we know it today. Besides this, the game is a masterpiece to me also due to its central theme of memes (academic meaning). It had me relfecting on the origin of information and how it spreads.
