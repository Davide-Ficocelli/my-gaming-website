About the game

Skyrim is an open-world fantasy RPG developed by Bethesda Game Studios, released in 2011. Set 200 years after Oblivion, players take the role of the Dragonborn - a hero with the power to absorb dragon souls. The game features a massive world with mountains, forests, dungeons, and cities to explore, alongside a deep character progression system with skills that improve through use.

Visit the official website

Key Features:

  • Massive open world with diverse environments
  • Dragon Shout magic system
  • Many side quests to be explored
  • Deep crafting and enchanting systems
  • Over 300 hand-crafted locations

Why I like it

What I love the most about Skyrim is its immersive world and the music it offers. Its music is so good I can use it while studying or working. The game allows for endless exploration and adventure, and if this weren't enough, mods are available in a large variety. Similarly to Elden Ring, you can decide your playstyle as you have many skill trees to choose from. Although it's no secret the game contains certain glitches like being able to steal items without getting caught by simply putting a basket on someone's head, these kind of glitches aren't annoying at all, instead they're funny to watch to me. All the more, Skyrim will always have a special place in my heart as it's a game I used to play a lot during my childhood.
